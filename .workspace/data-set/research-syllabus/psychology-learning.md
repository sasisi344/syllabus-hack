# Psychology-Based Learning Optimization

## 1. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT/認知行動療法)
- **Concept**: Challenging and changing cognitive distortions and behaviors to improve emotional regulation and personal coping strategies.
- **Application**:
    - **Cognitive Restructuring**: Identifying negative self-talk (e.g., "I'm not good at math") and reframing them with growth-oriented statements.
    - **Behavioral Experiments**: Testing different study methods and recording results to objectively find what works.
    - **Anxiety Management**: Using grounding techniques and exposure to difficult subjects to reduce academic avoidance.

## 2. Stoicism (ストア哲学)
- **Concept**: Focusing on what is within one's control and accepting what is not.
- **Application**:
    - **Dichotomy of Control (制御の二分法)**: Focusing energy on the *process* (study habits, hours spent) rather than the *outcome* (grades, rankings).
    - **Voluntary Discomfort**: Embracing challenging subjects as a way to build mental "fortitude" and resilience.
    - **Premeditatio Malorum (負の先取り)**: Anticipating potential setbacks (e.g., failing a test) to dampen the emotional impact and prepare a response.

## 3. Best Practices
- **If-Then Planning**: "If I feel like quitting, then I will finish just 3 more pages."
- **Self-Efficacy**: Building confidence through small, consistent wins (Mastery Experiences).
- **Journaling**: Daily reflection to process stress and refine learning strategies.
