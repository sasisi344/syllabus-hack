# Neuroscience-Based Learning Optimization

## 1. Neuroplasticity (神経可塑性)
- **Concept**: The brain's ability to reorganize itself by forming new neural connections. "Neurons that fire together, wire together."
- **Application**: 
    - **Retrieval Practice (想起練習)**: Actively recalling information instead of re-reading. This strengthens the neural pathways associated with the memory.
    - **Spaced Repetition (分散学習)**: Spacing out study sessions to allow for memory consolidation during sleep.
    - **Interleaving (連続学習)**: Mixing different topics to maintain novelty and trigger neuroplasticity through cognitive challenge.

## 2. Dopamine Management (ドーパミン管理)
- **Concept**: Dopamine is a neurotransmitter linked to motivation and reward. Overstimulation can lead to decreased focus.
- **Application (Dopamine Fasting/Detox)**:
    - **Focus Reset**: Strategically limiting highly stimulating activities (social media, etc.) during study windows.
    - **Task Selection**: Focusing on high-value, slow-reward tasks (meaningful study) to recalibrate the reward system.
    - **Environment**: Designing low-stimulus environments to prevent impulsive dopamine-seeking behavior.

## 3. Best Practices
- **Active Recall**: Use flashcards or blurting (writing everything you remember) to force retrieval.
- **Micro-Learning**: Break complex information into "chunks" to avoid cognitive overload.
- **Rest & Sleep**: Essential for "Pruning" (removing unnecessary connections) and consolidation of new neural pathways.
